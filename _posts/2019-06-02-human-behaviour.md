Philosophers, economists, and psychologists have long tried to answer questions on how we value things, and choose between options. Human behavior continues to defy the most subtle models of it, and the algorithms producing human behavior remain hidden in a black box. Behaviour shifts are constant and on the rise. [Technology acclerates it](https://techcrunch.com/2018/12/12/changing-consumer-behavior-is-the-key-to-unlocking-billion-dollar-businesses/).

Common sense suggests that we infer others' feelings from their appearance and actions, but we have a different, more direct route to our own feelings: direct perception or introspection. If we can be wrong about our own desires, then presumably many of our desires are activated unconsciously and operate unconsciously.

## How do we unravel the hidden complexity of our choices and values?

We now have neuroscientists directly measuring the neurons whose firing rates encode value and produce our choices. We know a lot more about the science of human motivation than you might think. Now we can peer directly into the black box of human motivation, and begin (dimly) to read our own source code.

The purpose of this document is not to argue for any of the conclusions presented within it. That would require writing a book, such as [Foundations of Neuroeconomic Analysis](https://www.amazon.com/Foundations-Neuroeconomic-Analysis-Paul-Glimcher/dp/0199744254/) and [Handbook of Reward and Decision ](https://www.amazon.com/Handbook-Reward-Decision-Making-Jean-Claude/dp/0123746205/).

*Human behaviour is complex and often hard to understand but motivation is simple, predictable, and can be trained using a reward system. Motivation can influence behaviour.*

## Folk Psychology

We undergo metabolism and cell growth, produce waste, maintain homeostasis, reproduce, move, communicate, and sometimes participate in pillow fights.

Processes like cell growth and breathing are *automatic*, while moving, communicating, and having pillow fights are *intentional* and *willed* that can be broadly classified as *actions*. A technical explaination of human actions can be folk psychology which posits that we humans have beliefs and desires, and that we are motivated to do what we believe will fulfill our desires.

Most of us use folk psychology every day to successfully predict human behavior.

### Single desire

You bought cookies last night and left them in the kitchen. You now desire a cookie. To fullfil this desire you are required to walk to the kitchen and put one in your mouth. Thus you are motivated to move to eat a cookie.

### Multiple desires

Suppose you have two desires, to eat a cookie and to remain sitting comfortably in the living room. If you ask your wife nicely, she will. So you are motivated to motivate another individual to fulfill two of your desires, i.e. eat a cookie and remain in the living room.

How it works: You know your wife desires to do nice things for you on occasion if they're not too much trouble, and you believe that, once you tell her you want a cookie, she'll believe she can be nice to you without much trouble if she brings you a cookie from the kitchen.

So, you predict that your wife will bring you a cookie when you ask her. So you ask her, the prediction was correct. The validation is you are happily eating a cookie on the sofa.
