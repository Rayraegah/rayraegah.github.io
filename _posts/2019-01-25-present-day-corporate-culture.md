Corporate culture has changed to where people are trained today to outpass each other and conduct political campaigns to isolate opposition (salience, political survival, working the ref, etc.) instead of handling conflict according to the principle. (e.g. Harvard negotiation project, getting to yes, principled negotiations, etc.)

The moment you recognize that the person you deal with is dissenting and manipulative is the moment you lost. That you can see the mechanism critically indicates that you have no power and that you can't be confident that you keep the ball in the air.

The typical response to this is, that's black and white thinking, people have different perspectives and views, which are valuable and legitimate. While this is usually true, different views are neither the problem nor new. It is a minority of people who exploit the acceptability and civility of others with a small set of negative tools and cluster in high-level organizations because they are motivated by their proximity to power, but without responsibility.
