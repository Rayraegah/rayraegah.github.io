During the project in Manhattan, Feynman and other people were asked to report what they would find in the next quarter. Since they were doing groundbreaking work, nobody had any idea how to respond, and this caused a lot of stress for the researchers.

Then they realized that they could hold down results worth three months and "estimate" their discoveries. In the end, management was very pleased with the predictable progress and scientists were free to do their job without hindrances.
